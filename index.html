Runway Selection Explained

Selecting the correct runway is crucial for safe takeoffs and landings. Pilots prefer to land and take off into the wind to reduce ground speed and increase control. The runway numbers correspond to their magnetic heading rounded to the nearest 10 degrees (e.g., Runway 22 is approximately 220°). To determine the best runway, calculate the difference between the wind direction and each runway's heading. The runway with the smallest angle difference (up to 180°) is the optimal choice.

Descent Calculation Explained

Knowing when to start your descent ensures a smooth approach and reduces the need for abrupt altitude changes. The basic formula to calculate the distance to start descent is:

Distance = (Current Altitude - Target Altitude) / (Descent Rate / Ground Speed)

Since speed is in knots (nautical miles per hour) and descent rate is in feet per minute, we adjust units accordingly:

  1. Calculate the altitude difference (in feet).
  2. Determine the time to descend using the descent rate (Time = Altitude Difference / Descent Rate).
  3. Convert speed to nautical miles per minute (Speed / 60).
  4. Calculate distance using Distance = Speed per Minute × Time.

By applying this calculation, you can determine the optimal distance to begin your descent for a smooth and safe approach.
